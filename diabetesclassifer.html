Overview

Through a bootcamp organized by UC Berkeley, I learned the fundamentals of data science and various statistical concepts while acquiring hands-on experience with Python libraries in the field. In order to demonstrate and further my learning, I worked on a capstone project that is able to classify a patientâ€™s susceptibility to diabetes.

To ensure the reliability of the provided big data, my first step involved cleaning and wrangling. Once the data was refined, I conducted exploratory data analysis, leveraging visualizations to unveil patterns and relationships. Subsequently, I divided the dataset into training and testing sets to train and evaluate the model, preventing overfitting and ensuring realistic performance assessment. In the final stage, I implemented a K-nearest neighbors (KNN) classifier, utilizing its proximity-based predictions of data points to assess a patient's likelihood of having diabetes.
