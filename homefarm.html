What is the problem?

As I strolled through my neighborhood, I noticed an abundance of delicious fruits and vegetable plants. However, it saddened me to observe that a significant portion of this homegrown produce was on the verge of decay, highlighting an unfortunate and avoidable waste of food.

What is my idea?

After seeing this wastage, an idea sprouted in my head: a mobile app, where individuals can share their surplus fruits and vegetables, enabling others to request and connect over shared produce, thereby fostering a culture of community and sustainability.

What is the strategy?

  • Create and design a mobile application to supply users with a platform to begin saving surplus produce
  • Long term: fully develop + release onto Google Play Store for people to use
  • Initially promote within own community with benefits
  • Possibly connect with a charity organization, such as Foothill Unity Center, to provide surplus produce to the needy

What steps have I taken?

  • Defined requirements and analyzed various possibilities for layouts
  • Over the course of four months, LEARNED all necessary tools to develop Android application
  • Started with basic design (front end), then added Firebase for backend (authentication, database, storage, and messaging)
  • Ensured all components/parts were functional

What does success look?

Success for this app lies in fostering community, sharing resources, and reducing food waste by connecting individuals with extra produce to those in need. While the app may not solve global food wastage or hunger, it empowers ordinary people to make a meaningful impact, providing an opportunity to collectively address these worrisome issues at a local level.
